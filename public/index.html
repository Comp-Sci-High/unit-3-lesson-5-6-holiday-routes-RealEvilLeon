About Eid‑Ul‑Adha

Eid‑Ul‑Adha, also called the Festival of Sacrifice, commemorates the willingness of Ibrahim (Abraham) to sacrifice his son in obedience to God. Families gather for prayers, charity, feasts, and reflection.

Common Traditions

Prayer

Special congregational prayers in the morning bring communities together.

Sacrifice & Sharing

Animals are sacrificed and meat is distributed among family, friends, and those in need.

Charity

Giving to the less fortunate is central — ensuring everyone can partake in the celebration.
